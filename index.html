About

Where code meets compassion, minds, machines, and medicine.

I was ten years old when I watched a movie inspired by Asimov's The Bicentennial Man — a robot searching for humanity, creating synthetic organs to save lives on his journey toward becoming imperfect, beautifully human.

That moment sparked something in me: a dream of creating technology that could heal from within. My love for science, problem-solving, and healthcare found its true purpose when I discovered biomedical engineering.

Suddenly everything made sense — here was where I could chase that childhood dream of synthetic organs and human-machine integration. Now I'm fueled by a burning passion for development and innovation, diving deep into VR, AI, and software that bridges the gap between human biology and technology.

Every line of code, every application I build, brings me closer to that ten-year-old's vision. I'm not just building healthcare applications — I'm chasing a dream that began with a robot who taught me that the most beautiful technology is the kind that makes us more human.
